RxFx

Prescription Drug Recommendations

Data

The data for RxFx come from openFDA, a repository of nearly 3 million adverse drug event reports since 2004.

Record submissions come primarily from consumers, physicians, pharmacists, and other health care professionals.

Recommendations

Prescription drugs are recommended based on the rank ordering of preferences. Individual weights are assigned to adverse effects by taking the inverse of their rank.

For example, if the ordering of side effects were 'Nausea' before 'Dizziness', thier rank orders would be '1' and '2', and thier respective weights would be '1/1' and '1/2'. This means that Dizziness would be considered one-half as important as Nausea as a side effect to be avoided.

Once weights are assigned to each side effect, they are multiplied against the probability of their occurence before being added together. Continuing with this example, suppose Drug A has a 0.5 probability of leading to Nausea, and a 0.2 probability of leading to Dizziness. The score for Drug A would then be (1/1 x 0.5) + (1/2 x 0.2) = 0.6. This is done for all of the drugs most commonly used to treat the specific condition, and the drug with the lowest score recieves the recommendation.
